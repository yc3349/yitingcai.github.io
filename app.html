Overview

WHAT’S THE PROBLEM

  • The new generation (Digital Natives) is wired differently from earlier generation (Digital Immigrants)due to the influence of technology, thus need to be educated differently.
  • What would be the efficient parenting be in the new digital era?
  • Parents needs to know the efficient way to intereact with their kids.

    • Research Findings

  • The absence of age-appropriate, ‘guided interaction’

    (Plowman, McPake & Stephen, 2008)

    by parents harms the language acquisition and development of traditional literacy.
  • The children most at risk of delayed language acquisition are those from low socio-economic backgrounds whose parents are not involved in their child’s use of media.

    (Brittman, Rutherford, Brown, and Unsworth, 2011)

  • When technology is used appropriately, apps can increase kid’s cognitive and social abilities.

    (Hernandez, 2014)

  • Some of the most popular/profitable YouTube channels take advantage of emerging mobile and tablet technologies to target very young children and infant (aged 0-5 years).

    (Burroughs, 2017)

  • Since preschoolers cognitive and functional abilities are still in the developmental stage, parents play a crucial role in fostering appropriate and safe digital.

    (Hernandez, 2014)

  • The technologies are also a part of larger parenting practices through the watching of televisual content by children on mobile phones and tablets.

    (Burroughs, 2017)

  • The involvement of parents and family members in the home is one of the keys to achieving success in schools.

    (Weems, Rogers, 2008)

  • Families can encourage interpersonal interactions and limit screen time by following guidelines: quantity, quality, location, together, screen free, and reflect.

    (Huisman, 2014)

    • Proposal Solution

    Empower parents on parenting through daily curated content (curated daily activity, bite-size knowledge, community forming/supporting)
